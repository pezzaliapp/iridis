## 🇬🇧 English

### Notice on the non-medical nature of Iridis

**Iridis is a demonstrative prototype for research and technical feasibility validation purposes.**

Iridis is not, and does not present itself as:

- a medical device pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR);
- a diagnostic tool;
- a substitute for medical evaluation, ophthalmological examination, or laboratory analysis;
- a tool approved for clinical use or therapeutic decisions.

Outputs produced by Iridis are **numerical estimates derived from colorimetric heuristics** applied to a photograph. Such estimates:

- have no diagnostic value;
- have not been clinically validated on a representative population;
- may vary significantly depending on lighting, camera quality, eye position, skin tone, and other uncontrolled variables.

### What to do if you are concerned about your health

Any suspicion of jaundice, anemia, cataract or other medical condition must be evaluated by a **qualified healthcare professional** (general practitioner, ophthalmologist, hematologist, as appropriate). Iridis does not substitute this evaluation in any form and must never be used to delay or avoid it.

### Privacy and biometric data processing

Eye imagery constitutes biometric data per art. 9 of EU Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR), a special category of personal data. The current Iridis prototype:

- processes images **entirely in the user's browser** (client-side);
- sends no images to any server;
- stores no images or derived data;
- uses no tracking cookies or analytics services.

If features requiring transmission or retention of biometric data are introduced in the future, they will be: (a) described in a dedicated privacy notice, (b) subject to explicit consent, (c) accompanied by documented DPIA.
